No Limit Texas Hold'em

Game Overview

The most popular poker variant where players can bet any amount up to their entire stack at any time.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 2 hole cards dealt face down
  • 5 community cards are dealt face up in the center
  • Players make the best 5-card hand using any combination of their 2 cards and the 5 community cards

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-flop: After receiving hole cards
  • Flop: After first 3 community cards
  • Turn: After 4th community card
  • River: After 5th community card

Betting Structure

  • Players can bet any amount up to their entire stack
  • Minimum bet is the size of the big blind
  • Minimum raise must be at least the size of the previous bet/raise

Limit Texas Hold'em

Game Overview

Similar to No Limit Hold'em but with fixed betting limits that restrict the amount you can bet or raise.

Betting Structure

  • Small bet used pre-flop and on the flop
  • Big bet (2x small bet) used on turn and river
  • Maximum of 4 bets per player per round (bet, raise, re-raise, cap)

Example: $2/$4 Limit

  • Pre-flop & Flop: $2 bets and raises
  • Turn & River: $4 bets and raises
  • Small blind: $1, Big blind: $2

Strategic Differences

  • More mathematical and less psychological than No Limit
  • Can't bluff opponents out of pots as easily
  • Pot odds calculations are more straightforward

Razz (Seven Card Stud Low)

Game Overview

A lowball variant of Seven Card Stud where the lowest hand wins. Aces are always low.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 7 cards total
  • First 2 cards dealt face down, 3rd card face up
  • Cards 4, 5, 6 dealt face up
  • 7th card dealt face down

Betting Rounds

  • 3rd Street: Player with highest door card brings in
  • 4th-6th Streets: Player with best low hand showing acts first
  • 7th Street: Player with best low hand showing acts first

2-7 Single Draw

Game Overview

A lowball draw game where players try to make the worst possible poker hand. The best hand is 2-3-4-5-7 (not suited).

Dealing & Play

  • Each player receives 5 cards face down
  • First betting round
  • Players can discard and draw new cards (0-5 cards)
  • Second and final betting round
  • Showdown

Hand Rankings (Best to Worst)

  • Worst hand: A-A-A-A-A

Strategy Notes

  • Standing pat (not drawing) can be a powerful bluff
  • Drawing one card suggests a strong made hand
  • Position is crucial due to betting structure

Omaha Hi-Lo (Omaha 8 or Better)

Game Overview

A split pot game where the highest and lowest hands share the pot. Players receive 4 hole cards and must use exactly 2 of them.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 4 hole cards
  • 5 community cards dealt (flop, turn, river)
  • Must use exactly 2 hole cards and exactly 3 community cards

Low Hand Qualification

  • Low hand must be 8 or better (A-2-3-4-5 through 8-7-6-5-4)
  • Aces are low for low hands
  • Straights and flushes don't count against low hands
  • Best low: A-2-3-4-5 (wheel)

Pot Distribution

  • If qualifying low exists: High hand wins half, low hand wins half
  • If no qualifying low: High hand wins entire pot
  • Same player can win both high and low (scoop)

Omaha Hi (Pot Limit Omaha)

Game Overview

Similar to Texas Hold'em but players receive 4 hole cards and must use exactly 2 of them. Usually played with pot limit betting.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 4 hole cards
  • 5 community cards dealt (flop, turn, river)
  • Must use exactly 2 hole cards and exactly 3 community cards

Pot Limit Betting

  • Can bet up to the size of the pot
  • Pot size = current pot + all bets in current round + your call
  • More action than limit, less swingy than no limit

Example Pot Calculation

Pot: $100, Opponent bets $50
Your maximum raise: $100 + $50 + $50 = $200
(pot + their bet + your call)

Strategy Notes

  • Much more action than Hold'em due to 4 cards
  • Draws are more common and powerful
  • Position is extremely important
  • Nuts and near-nuts play much bigger role

Seven Card Stud Hi-Lo (Stud 8 or Better)

Game Overview

A split pot variant of Seven Card Stud where high and low hands share the pot. Low hand must qualify with 8 or better.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 7 cards total
  • First 2 cards face down, 3rd card face up
  • Cards 4, 5, 6 dealt face up
  • 7th card dealt face down

Betting Rounds

  • 3rd Street: Lowest door card brings in
  • 4th-6th Streets: Highest showing hand acts first
  • 7th Street: Highest showing hand acts first

Low Hand Qualification

  • Must have 5 cards 8 or lower
  • Aces are low for low hands
  • Straights and flushes don't hurt low hands
  • Best low: A-2-3-4-5

Pot Distribution

  • If qualifying low exists: pot is split
  • If no qualifying low: high hand wins all
  • Same player can scoop both halves

Seven Card Stud

Game Overview

A classic poker game where players receive 7 cards each and make the best 5-card high hand. No community cards.

Dealing

  • Each player receives 7 cards total
  • First 2 cards dealt face down (hole cards)
  • 3rd card dealt face up (door card)
  • 4th, 5th, 6th cards dealt face up
  • 7th card dealt face down

Betting Rounds

  • 3rd Street: Player with lowest door card brings in
  • 4th Street: Player with highest showing hand acts first
  • 5th Street: Player with highest showing hand acts first
  • 6th Street: Player with highest showing hand acts first
  • 7th Street: Player with highest showing hand acts first

Bring-in Rules

  • Player with lowest door card must bring in
  • Suits break ties: clubs (lowest), diamonds, hearts, spades (highest)
  • Bring-in is typically 1/3 of small bet

Strategy Elements

  • Memory of folded cards is crucial
  • Reading opponents' boards
  • Starting hand selection based on door cards
  • Calculating live cards for draws
